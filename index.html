About Me

After completing a degree in psychology and sociology at the University of Brighton, and gaining experience working with children, I decided to pursue a career in teaching. I completed a Postgraduate Certificate in Education at the University of East London. After a couple of years in teaching, having been recognised for having a particular aptitude for maths teaching, I completed a further Postgraduate Certificate in Maths Specialist Teaching. Over the years, maths has become a passion of mine and it has been great to use these skills on my coding journey.

During my time working in education, I have taken on various leadership roles that eventually led me to become an assistant headteacher. I started as Maths Lead at a school in Greenwich, focusing on improving maths teaching across the school. When I moved to a school in Tower Hamlets, I became the Pupil Voice Lead, where I combined my passion for the environment with giving students a say in school matters and the world around them. In this role, I worked closely with the student council to ensure students' voices were heard. I also promoted the Sustainable Development Goals and the United Nations Charter on the Rights of the Child. One of my proudest moments was leading the student council in a campaign to reduce unsafe parking and pollution outside the school, where our efforts led to the students becoming the youngest individuals to present a petition at a local council meeting. Within this role, I also led the school in earning the Unicef Rights Respecting Gold Award.

Alongside my various roles in leadership, teaching also gave me my first experiences of coding, something I hadn't been taught in my own school career. Over the years, I have used a range of different programs such as Scratch to support pupils in taking their first foray into coding. Together, we completed a range of projects such as creating platform games, working together to solve problems and make the different parts of the game work in the desired way. It was great to see all pupils, even those who were often disengaged, find success and truly enjoy learning.

Teaching at the primary level let me use and grow a wide range of skills and interests, from my love for the environment and art to maths, sports, and computing. It has also enabled me to further develop other skills essential for my future career, through mentoring new teachers, overcoming challenges, presenting ideas to a range of audiences, working successfully in a range of different teams, leading a large team as an assistant headteacher and effecting change in a work-place. I have a wide range of passions and interests, and I'm excited to find a position where I can put them all to good use.
