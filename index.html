About Me

I'm a software developer with 15 years experience working as a teacher and leader in education. I hold a degree in Applied Psychology and Sociology, as well as a Postgraduate Certificate in Education and a Postgraduate Certificate in Mathematics Specialist Teaching.

In my most recent role in education, I worked as an assistant headteacher at a large inner-London school where I was responsible for the quality of teaching and learning for a third of the school, comprising approximately 30 members of staff and 270 pupils. I have also worked as a Pupil Voice Lead, where I led on environmental education and human rights. In this role, I championed environmental issues and led the school in earning the Unicef Rights Respecting Gold Award

My time as a teacher has given me skills in communication, mentoring, time management and prioritisation and also gave me my first taste of software development, when teaching children how to code. I have wanted to transition into software development for a long time and finally took the leap this year. I have completing a great deal of self-guided learning, building proficiency in JavaScript, HTML and CSS. I am now enrolled on the Founders and Coders Software Development bootcamp where I am honing my skills in JavaScript and other areas such as git workflow and testing. So far, I have successfully collaborated on a number of projects which can be viewed in the 'Projects' section.

My short term ambition is to earn a place on an apprenticeship program where I can further develop my technical skills and put them into practice in real-world projects. In the future, I would love to work projects which can positively impact the planet and future generations, from education apps, to technology which supports sustainability and the environment.
